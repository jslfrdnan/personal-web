Danau Tondano


This is the largest lake in North Sulawesi. Located in the Tondano region, it is known as Lake Tondano.

To reach this stunning destination, visitors need to travel about 50 minutes, covering a distance of approximately 40 kilometers from the city of Manado. The journey takes you through beautiful scenery, and once you arrive, you’ll be invited to explore the lake’s shoreline, where you can soak in the breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and hills.

Lake Tondano is embraced by three mountains and one hill: Mount Lembean, Mount Kaweng, Mount Masarang, and Tampusu Hill. Among them, the most prominent is Mount Kaweng, rising majestically from the lake’s edge, a striking sight that dominates the landscape.

From the lakeside, fishing enthusiasts will find the perfect spot to indulge their hobby. Lake Tondano is renowned for its abundance of freshwater fish, with species like tilapia and sepat being the most common in this 4,000-hectare lake.

The water stored in the lake also plays a vital role in supplying the needs of the surrounding communities. Along the shores, you may notice neatly arranged fish farms operated by local fishermen, which help keep the fish within the area while supporting local livelihoods.

Behind the vastness of Lake Tondano lies an old legend—a tale of love and tragedy. According to local folklore, the lake was formed by the eruption of a volcano, triggered by a forbidden romance.

A young couple, whose love was not approved by their families, decided to run away. They fled deep into the forest near Mount Kaweng. But breaking the taboo of marrying against their parents’ wishes had dire consequences. Mount Kaweng erupted violently, creating a massive crater in its wake. That crater, over time, filled with water and became what we now know as Lake Tondano.
